Bad Chunking

  • The general idea of this is that users tend to forget what they're reading when they're met with a large block of text, most of which seems dull and uninteresting.

  • Splitting it up might make it easier. At least in this way, the users more or less have a choice in what they want to read by providing topic headers.

  • In this case, I can't hover over anything in order to access specific information. Imagine this being a page of FAQs. I don't think anyone would want to read through the entire thing to find one question.

  • This page has no interaction on it, and it's one of the bad examples of chunking: All the information is clustered into one boring heap and lacks any interesting qualities.
